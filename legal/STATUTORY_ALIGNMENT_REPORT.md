## VICFM (Vendor Contracts) Alignment

### Sole Source Procurement

**Statutory Issues**:
- **41 U.S.C. § 3301**: Full and open competition requirement
- **41 U.S.C. § 3303**: Exceptions to competition requirements
- **41 U.S.C. § 3304**: Justification for noncompetitive procedures

**Alignment Analysis**:
Non-competitive awards without proper justification violate procurement statutes and raise equal protection concerns under *Village of Willowbrook v. Olech*.

**Interpretive Canons Applied**:
- Clear Statement Rule: Waivers of competitive requirements need explicit authorization
- Equal Protection Scrutiny: Arbitrary vendor selection lacks rational basis

**Constitutional Risk**: Equal Protection Clause (Fourteenth Amendment)

## Post-Loper Bright Framework

### Impact on Statutory Interpretation

**Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo** (144 S. Ct. 2244, 2024):

**Key Changes**:
- ❌ **Chevron deference overruled**: No deference to agency statutory interpretations
- ✅ **Independent judicial review**: Courts exercise independent judgment on all statutory questions
- ✅ **APA § 706 reaffirmed**: Courts decide "all relevant questions of law"

**JIM Integration**:
- Enhanced scrutiny for agency interpretations
- Greater focus on statutory text and structure
- Increased weight on clear statement requirements

**Practical Impact**:
Agency actions now face heightened judicial review. Statutory ambiguities no longer defer to agencies, requiring clearer congressional authorization.
