COUNTY OF TULARE — COUNTY ADMINISTRATOR'S OFFICE
Technology Governance Assessment — Public Safety Monitoring Program

ASSESSMENT DATE: 2024-01-20
PREPARED BY: County Administrator's Office, Compliance Division

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
This assessment documents governance readiness for the planned county-wide deployment
of the SecureTech Solutions public safety monitoring platform.

DEPLOYMENT SCOPE:
The County intends to deploy surveillance infrastructure at 47 locations across the
county. Deployment is planned to commence 2024-03-01 following board authorization
expected at the 2024-02-06 Board of Supervisors meeting.

GOVERNANCE GAP FINDINGS:
The following policy instruments have not been adopted as of the date of this assessment:

  1. Countywide Surveillance Governance Policy — No policy exists governing the
     operation, oversight, or auditing of the proposed surveillance network.

  2. Data Retention and Destruction Schedule — No formal schedule has been adopted
     for retention, archiving, or deletion of video, license plate, or acoustic data.

  3. Inter-Agency Data Sharing Protocol — The County has not established a policy
     governing whether or how collected data may be shared with municipalities,
     state agencies, or federal law enforcement.

  4. Public Transparency and Accountability Framework — No public hearing has been
     held and no public disclosure has been made describing the planned surveillance
     capability or its legal basis.

  5. Independent Civil Liberties Review — No independent legal or civil liberties
     review of the program has been conducted.

RISK CLASSIFICATION: CRITICAL

RECOMMENDATION:
All five governance instruments must be adopted before system activation. The scale
of county-wide deployment and absence of any data governance framework creates
substantial constitutional and statutory risk.

PEER JURISDICTION FINDING:
Comparable governance gap findings have been documented at the City of Riverside
and City of Lakewood, both of which are also contracting with SecureTech Solutions
for public safety monitoring infrastructure. The recurrence of this pattern across
three independent jurisdictions suggests a systemic gap in vendor due diligence
and pre-contract governance review practices.
