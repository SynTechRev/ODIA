CITY OF RIVERSIDE — INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY DEPARTMENT
Internal Governance Assessment — Public Safety Technology Deployment

ASSESSMENT DATE: 2024-02-15
PREPARED BY: IT Department, Policy Compliance Unit

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
This assessment identifies governance gaps associated with the planned deployment of the
SecureTech Solutions public safety monitoring platform.

CAPABILITY DEPLOYMENT STATUS:
The SecureTech Solutions platform is scheduled to go live on 2024-04-01. As of the date
of this assessment, the following governance instruments have NOT yet been established:

  1. Data Retention Policy — No policy exists governing how long video feeds, license
     plate data, and pedestrian analytics will be stored or when they will be purged.

  2. Access Control Policy — No documented policy governs which City employees may
     access real-time feeds or historical data exports.

  3. Public Disclosure Notice — The City has not published a public notice describing
     the nature, scope, or legal authority for the surveillance program.

  4. Civil Liberties Impact Assessment — No independent review has been conducted
     to assess the program's impact on First or Fourth Amendment-protected activity.

RISK CLASSIFICATION: HIGH

RECOMMENDATION:
IT Department recommends that deployment be conditioned on adoption of a comprehensive
surveillance governance policy. Deployment without such a policy creates legal exposure
under California Civil Code and the California Consumer Privacy Act.

REFERENCE:
Similar governance gap findings have been documented in peer jurisdictions including
the City of Lakewood and County of Tulare, which are proceeding with comparable
technology deployments under the same vendor contract framework.
